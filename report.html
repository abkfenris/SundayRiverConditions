Morning Report
Last Updated: December 28, 2023 | 2:15 PM

Mother Nature gave us another day of comfortable temps and soft turns. We truly appreciate everyone's kindness and patience during the construction process as we work to repair damage from last week's storm.

We’re still in a snowmaking pause, so we can expect the same lineup of trails for tomorrow, but our grooming will be laying stripes on everything. There is a prolonged period of colder temperatures in the incoming forecast, so our snowmaking team will have ample time to utilize over 10,000 gallons of water per minute as we work towards resurfacing and expansion. Our snowguns will be running as soon as that cold snap hits, so think cold.

The New Year is almost here, and with it, our New Year’s Eve events.  Stay out for our extended Twilight skiing, catch a fire dancing performance, and watch the fireworks slopeside. Want an indoor activity? Bring the kids to their own New Year’s Eve Party in the Mahoosuc Room, and then ring in the New Year with Skosh at the Foggy Goggle. Keep an eye on the River Guide for all current hours, and check out our Christmas Vacation Week page for a full list of this week's events.

Next Steps

Thanks to our Mountain Ops crews and countless contractors, we are excited to open the Peak Lodge, as well as the Mountain Room, tomorrow, December 29. Access will be via the Spruce Peak Triple to Sirius, Aludra, then Light's Out.

In addition to the Peak Lodge opening, we are planning to open the Chondola this Sunday, December 31. As construction wraps up in South Ridge and snowmaking comes back online, we are expecting to load the gondola cabins only for the first few days.

Looking ahead, our goal is to be skiing in the Jordan Bowl on Friday, January 5. This will include ski-in, ski-out access to the Jordan Hotel. The Alpeniglu is also scheduled to re-open on Friday, as well as the Outpost, for convenient slopeside snacks and beverages. Snowmaking temps will be crucial to meeting this goal, but the forecast looks promising, and our snowmakers are raring to get back to what they do best.

Thank you to everyone who has supported Sunday River through this recovery process. We wouldn’t be here without everyone who helped.

We'll see you out there!

- Team Snow
