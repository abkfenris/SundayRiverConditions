December 5, 2021

Ho-Ho-Holy-Moly we have a great day for you! After another full night of snowmaking and grooming, corduroy trails await you this calm morning. The Chondola is now open, spinning from 8AM-3:45PM. New park features on Upper Sunday Punch and Wonderland make for an adventurous day. The Foggy Goggle opens for the first time this season, today from 11AM-4:30PM.

What does Santa and snowguns have in common? They’re out at the slopes today for the 21st Annual Santa Sunday event! Come see over 200 riders dress up as Santa, ringing in this holiday season, in collaboration with the River Fund. If you’d like to see all the Santas on one slope, watch them shred down Broadway trail around 11AM. Look forward to an exciting afternoon!

All lift tickets need to be purchased online in advance. Have your RFID from last year? You can head straight to the lift! Skier services are available in both the Barker and South Ridge Lodges, as well as shuttle service between these lodges and the hotels while the lifts spin. We are now accepting cashless forms of payment only. Please be sure to have a gift card, resort charge, credit card, or debit card available for on-site purchases.

Around the Resort

The South Ridge Food Court is now open, along with the Barker Food Court, Waffle Cabin in Barker Basin, Northern Lights Cafe at the Jordan Hotel, Camp at the Grand Summit Hotel, and Shipyard Brew Haus at White Cap Lodge. Foggy Goggle opens for the season today! Plus, get your retail therapy in at Sunday River Sports, and your relaxation on at the Jordan Spa. Please note that the Tune Shop is closed this weekend, but you can pick up skis at the repair counter in Sunday River Sports.

For the latest updates whenever you visit us, head to the River Guide.

COVID-19 Protocols

Though we are no longer requiring guests to wear masks in general areas, we suggest masking indoors. However, some services may require you to wear a mask, so be sure to have one on hand. Please make sure to read through our COVID Winter Guide before visiting the resort to understand our operating plans and how they may affect you. Remember, we're all in this together.
