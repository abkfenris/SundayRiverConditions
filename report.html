MONDAY, FEBRUARY 15

Mondays aren't so bad when we get to spend it on the slopes. Add in a holiday and an incoming snowstorm, which could drop up to 9" of snow tonight and into tomorrow, and you can just start calling us Monday River.

Lifts open at 8AM every day this week. Get here early for morning corduroy on over 80 trails or hop into the woods over on Jordan Bowl to search for secret stashes. If you’re just learning, start the day at South Ridge for the best beginner terrain. If you’re staying at the Grand Summit, there are also great green trails off of Little White Cap Quad for some mellow turns. Twilight skiing continues through Saturday for Presidents’ Week with access to the Chondola and South Ridge Express until 6:30PM. As you plan your trip, keep in mind lift tickets are limited and days are close to selling out, so plan ahead if you want to hit the slopes this week. Or book lodging with us and guarantee access to full-day lift tickets, including one-night stays through Thursday, February 18, at the Jordan Hotel and Snow Cap Inn. Please note that kids ski school lessons are now available to book online with limited availability.

We require online lift ticket purchase and set limits on the number of tickets sold for each day. This includes full-day, beginner, Threedom additional days, and Passholder Friend tickets. We only expect these limits to affect peak dates, but encourage everyone to buy early to ensure access on the dates you want. Season passes, Ikon passes, and lift ticket packs remain direct-to-lift with no restrictions or reservations.

Fast and contactless lift ticket pickup is available at our PUBs (Pick Up Boxes) at base lodges and hotels. Just scan the barcode at the bottom of your confirmation email and your RFID cards will print with your lift tickets or ticket packs loaded. For best results turn off dark mode on your phone if you use it and avoid email apps that use dark backgrounds.

Please remember to take a self-administered Health Screening and visit our COVID Winter Guide before each trip to stay up-to-date on safety protocols and state guidelines.
