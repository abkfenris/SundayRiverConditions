Shifting Seasons

September 7, 2022

It's September, folks. Can you believe it? We certainly can't. But yet, there's a chill in the air... a whisper of autumn that will lead us full steam ahead into our favorite season. Winter will be here before we know it. With that in mind, secure your New England Pass for season-long access to the most dependable snow in New England, or check out our ticket packs for even bigger savings. Brand new for this year, check out the Maine Pack for 4 days to use at Sunday River, Sugarloaf, and Shawnee Peak. While you're browsing, don't forget to check out Lift Tickets, Lodging, and lessons through our Snowsports School. We'll be sliding on snow in no time, so plan now and save big.

September marks the last full month of operation at the Sunday River Golf Club! Tee times are open, so book early to secure the lowest rates. Many of our Saturdays are close to fully sold out. Stay & Play packages are also available to save even more on your next favorite golf vacation. These packages are only available by phone, so call (877) 346-0451 to book. 

Looking for a casual way to enjoy the beautiful scenery of Western Maine?  Join us Saturday & Sunday from 10AM - 4PM for a ride up to the top of North Peak on our Chondola. Enjoy sweeping views of the Mahoosuc Mountains, including Maine's fourth highest peak, Old Speck. Scenic lift rides are dog, kid, and grandparent-friendly, so pack up the whole family. Tickets can be pre-purchased here. 

South Ridge has a few new features including Sunny's beer garden and outdoor activities like corn hole, badminton, and giant Jenga.    

Weekly Trail Report 

September 1, 2022

It's a great time to hit the trails. Head to the top of one of Sunday River's peaks for a cool breeze and phenomenal views of Western Maine, the White Mountains, and the Mahoosuc Mountains. Don't forget to bring plenty of water, sunscreen, a hat, suitable footwear, and some salty snacks to replace what you sweat out. Water bottle filling stations are located in South Ridge Lodge, as well as the hotels! Heavy rains have taken a toll on our trails. Please wear sturdy walking shoes and be prepared for mud!

Trails E and A are open from North Peak, but with a little detour; head to Second Mile and take a left up Three Mile to connect. If you’re not sure of your route, please check with the Chondola operators to make sure you have a safe plan. Due to construction, be on the lookout for additional trail closure signage this week including  North Peak and the bottom of the Spruce Triple. 

Construction continues on North Peak, which has impacted our trail conditions - please be wary of large holes and don't hike alone if you are unsure of your abilities. Expect frequent changes to access routes to trails H (kid’s Adventure Trail) and E (Summit Traverse) from the top of the Chondola. If you are having trouble finding your way, please ask one of our knowledgeable lift operators or patrollers and they will be able to assist you! 

Please adhere to all posted signs and warnings. Do not cross orange ropes or enter areas beyond “no trespassing” or “trail closed due to construction” signs. There are plenty of tables and chairs around North Peak lodge for you to enjoy a picnic lunch or sunny siesta with a view, but please remember to dispose of your trash and recycling at the top or bottom of the lift.

As you head to South Ridge base area, you may notice we have begun construction on Barker Mountain Rd. During the week, Monday - Friday, there will be limited access depending where the crew is working. Most access will be allowed via Cascade Hotel Hill Dr. Weekend traffic will flow as normal.

If you are injured or need assistance at any point, please contact Patrol at (207)-824-5350 or visit our First Aid Clinic in the bottom of the South Ridge Lodge next to Betty's Treats and Treasures.
