Morning Report
Last Updated: November 21, 2023 | 7:05 AM

The mountain sure looks good covered in white. Mother Nature joined our snowmakers in their efforts last night, dropping 4" of fresh snow across the mountain. Snow is forecasted to continue through the early afternoon, with the potential for another 2-4" of snow to fall throughout the day. Welcome to winter!

For today, we're planning on taking our first steps toward expansion. Excalibur is scheduled to join the trail count today, pending final approvals from patrol. If it opens, Excalibur will be ungroomed, natural terrain for experts only. Lollapalooza received a last-night groom, so fresh corduroy can be expected throughout the morning, but it is expected to bump up with the additional snow throughout the day. Rogue Angel received an early groom prior to the snowfall, making that your destination for some fresh turns.

In the world of terrain parks, we are planning to have a 4-feature park located at the bottom of the Excalibur. This will primarily be a hike park but will be available for ski access via Excalibur when patrol drops the rope. 

Please note that early-season, variable terrain will be present on all trails. Lastly, don't forget to check out our Early Season Guide.

As we approach Thanksgiving, we're kicking off our Cyber Sale, with deep discounts on lodging, lift tickets, ticket packs, and other deals. If you've been holding off planning your next favorite vacation, the time is now to lock it in and save.

For those needing ticket assistance, a Guest Service station is located in the bottom terminal of the Jordan 8. All Ikon Pass holders who need assistance must visit the Welcome Center in the South Ridge Base Lodge. Additionally, from now until December 1, all rentals for Type 2 skiers and above, can be made, in person only, at the Sunday River Rental Shop. The equipment rental shop is located in the tunnel at the South Ridge base area.  

Locke Mountain will continue to serve as the resort's dedicated early-season training venue for our partners Gould Academy and Bates College, as well as for pre-booked ski teams only. All public access to snow surfaces will be through the Jordan Bowl. 

Please note that as crews work to expand the resort, all uphill travel is prohibited. The current Uphill Route Status can be found at the bottom of the Mountain Report. Also, all construction areas remain off-limits. Thank you in advance for allowing our crews the space to safely expand our terrain and complete the last steps of exciting new projects.

As always, keep an eye here for the latest info on our lift and trail status, as well as any other operational updates.

Think cold!

- Team Snow
