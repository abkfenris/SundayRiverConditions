Morning Report

Happy Saturday! If you've peeked at the forecast, you'll know we're in for a treat today. Last night and this morning stayed on the colder side, which was welcomed for our team of groomers who were able to get out earlier to give the mountain its daily refresh. They hit their usual slate of trails, with the addition of Merrill Hill, leaving perfect stripes behind. The Tempest headwall has been smoothed out this morning as well. Some of the gusty winds from yesterday are still hanging around this morning, but are expected to die down as the day goes on. Please keep an eye on the mountain report for the latest operational updates as winds may impact lifts. The snow will be firm to start the day, but with endless sun and temps hanging around the 40's, it should soften up nicely this afternoon. 

This weekend is packed with awesome Spring events. Pond-a-palooza starts at 11AM in South Ridge, with free registration starting at 9AM in the Welcome Center. After watching the competitors make a splash in grandiose fashion, keep the party going at our second Spring Apres Concert from 1-6PM slopeside at South Ridge. We've also got some Easter activities and the Passholder Appreciation lunch on tap for Sunday. For all of the events through the rest of the year, check out the full schedule here.

As we wind down for the season be sure to check out our Spring operations schedule for all of the info on what lifts are spinning and when.

See you out there!
-Team Snow

Mountain Notes:

If you're looking to get over to Jordan from South Ridge, ski on over to Spruce Peak Triple and take Sirius > Aludra > Cyclone > State Fair. Kansas will bring you back from Jordan to South Ridge when you're ready to head back East.    

Spring brings with it some of the best conditions of the year, but also the most variable condition. Please note that trail conditions may vary and change rapidly throughout the day.
