Afternoon Report
Last Updated: December 30, 2023 | 1:34 PM

Today’s the day, folks. Four inches of natural snow fell this morning and temps are cooling down this afternoon. How were those turns out there?

The moment we’ve all been waiting for: snowmaking is going back online tonight. Our snowmaking team is planning on running our system at 100% capacity, and they’ll be making snow everywhere they can for as long as they can. New and resurfaced terrain is in our future. Our grooming team will be hitting all of our currently open terrain, and potentially new terrain as well. Keep an eye on this report for the most updated information.

Today, head over to the Jordan Spa for a Sips & Sweets event. Browse the boutique and get to know the spa team while enjoying some complimentary prosecco from 4-6PM. For some live music, head to the Foggy Goggle from 3-6PM to listen to Fast Times or Shipyard from 2-5PM for Justin Carver. If you’re more of a late-night music lover, Stream Reggae is playing at Shipyard from 7-10PM. Find all the details on the Après and Nightlife page. 2024 is fast approaching, and with it, New Year’s Eve. We have a kids-only party, a 21+ party, fireworks, and more. All of the details are on our New Year's Eve page, and be sure to check the River Guide for all updated hours.

Next Steps

Crews have been hard at work repairing storm damage that has affected the Chondola re-opening. Upon further evaluation, the Chondola will remain closed Sunday, December 31, but is tentatively planned to re-open on Monday, January 1.

Looking ahead, our goal is to be skiing in the Jordan Bowl on Friday, January 5. This will include ski-in, ski-out access to the Jordan Hotel. The Alpeniglu is also scheduled to re-open on Friday, as well as the Outpost, for convenient slopeside snacks and beverages. Snowmaking temps will be crucial to meeting this goal, but the forecast looks promising, and our snowmakers are ready to get back to what they do best.

Think cold, and we'll see you out there!
