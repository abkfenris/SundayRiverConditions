January 29, 2023 | 1:30 PM

Afternoon Report

The midday snow flurries are sticking around, turning our mountains into a winter wonderland. Snow should begin accumulating in the early afternoon, adding a nice little layer on top of our base by the end of the day. In the morning, all should be calm, with mild winds and cloud-filled skies. Temps will range from the mid-20s to mid-30s, creating very comfortable ski conditions for the end of January. By the last chair tomorrow, there may be another round of snow flurries going into the night.

Our Mountain Ops team is back on the clock tonight, making snow on closed terrain and sending snowcats all over our eight peaks to smooth out skied-up moguls and resurface the trails with beautiful corduroy stripes.

It's looking like another great week of skiing and riding here in western Maine as winter has arrived, bringing colder temps and the possibility of more snow later this Week.

 See you out there!
-Team Snow
