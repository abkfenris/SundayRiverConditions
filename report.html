Afternoon Report
Last Updated: January 20, 2024 | 1:41 PM

It's cold out there, but the skiing is excellent. Groomers, natural and man-made powder stashes, and snow flurries; what more could we ask for? 716 acres of skiable terrain and 17 lifts make for a great day of skiing and riding, and there's more of that in store for tomorrow. 

It will be a little bit warmer tomorrow, with highs in the low twenties in the base areas and low teens up top. It'll stay cold throughout the night, and our snowmakers will be finishing up on this set of trails before moving elsewhere on the mountain. Our grooming team will be laying stripes on the usual trails, as well as pushing out trails with snowmaking on it, like Jungle Road and Cascades. We're expecting corduroy on about half of our terrain tomorrow. Be sure to check back here for the most updated trail and lift info. 

The lifts may stop, but the fun does not. Stick around for twilight skiing from 4-6PM tonight off of the Chondola and the South Ridge Express, and then watch the fireworks in the South Ridge base area at 7PM. Stop by the Jordan Spa for Sips & Sweets from 4-5:30PM this evening, and browse the boutique with complimentary prosecco and sweets. Catch some live music this afternoon and this evening, as well; Fast Times will be at the Foggy Goggle from 3-6PM, Kevin Roper will be at the Barker Bar from 2-5PM, and the Shipyard will have Henry Lowery from 2-5PM and the Don Campbell Band from 7-10PM. Tonight is also the last opportunity for a one-night stay at the Jordan Hotel, so don't let that deal slip away. 

Think snow!

- Team Snow
