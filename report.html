Morning Report
Last Updated: December 29, 2023 | 6:42 AM

Today we have temperatures in the mid-thirties, but think cold: it looks like temps will start to drop a little throughout the day. We have the same lineup of trails as yesterday, and all 166 acres of open terrain got a fresh groom last night. The Barker 6, South Ridge Express, Locke Triple, Spruce Triple, White Cap Quad, and two Surface lifts will all be spinning today. We truly appreciate everyone's patience and kindness during the construction process as we repair damage from last week's storm.  

We will continue to have crossing guards posted at Outta Here and Lower Lazy River to assist with travel between Barker and South Ridge. We appreciate everyone’s patience with our active construction as we continue repairs.

With only a couple of days left in 2023, it’s time to start making those New Year’s Eve plans. We have a whole host of events, from a kids-only party in the Mahoosuc Room to a 21+ party in the Foggy Goggle with Skosh. Enjoy extended night skiing, and then stick around for the fireworks. For all event details, check out our Christmas Vacation Week page, and take a look at the River Guide for a full list of current hours.

Next Steps

Thanks to our Mountain Ops crews and countless contractors, we are excited to open the Peak Lodge, as well as the Mountain Room, today, December 29. Access will be via the Spruce Peak Triple to Sirius, Aludra, then Light's Out.

In addition to the Peak Lodge opening, we are planning to open the Chondola this Sunday, December 31. As construction wraps up in South Ridge and snowmaking comes back online, we are expecting to load the gondola cabins only for the first few days.

Looking ahead, our goal is to be skiing in the Jordan Bowl on Friday, January 5. This will include ski-in, ski-out access to the Jordan Hotel. The Alpeniglu is also scheduled to re-open on Friday, as well as the Outpost, for convenient slopeside snacks and beverages. Snowmaking temps will be crucial to meeting this goal, but the forecast looks promising, and our snowmakers are raring to get back to what they do best.

Thank you to everyone who has supported Sunday River through this recovery process. We wouldn’t be here without everyone who helped.

We'll see you out there!

- Team Snow
