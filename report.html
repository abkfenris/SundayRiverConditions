THURSDAY, FEBRUARY 18

Don’t press snooze this morning, there is soft corduroy ready for carving. Last night the groomers prowled the slopes as they pressed out pristine stripes. Hop on first chair at 8AM to make your mark across the blank canvas. Enjoy the peeks of sunshine today before the snow rolls in tonight. It looks like we could get a nice refresh on the slopes tomorrow with a couple of inches of snow, lining us up for another stellar weekend at the mountain. For some fun easy carving today, check out trails like Wildfire, Lazy River, Northern Lights, and Lollapalooza. If you’re just learning, we suggest starting the day at South Ridge for the best beginner terrain. Work up your confidence with the different levels of surface lifts before hoping on the South Ridge Express.

Twilight skiing will continue tonight through Saturday for Presidents’ Week with access to the Chondola and South Ridge Express until 6:30PM. As you plan your trip, keep in mind lift tickets are limited and days are close to selling out, so plan ahead if you want to hit the slopes this week. Or book lodging with us and guarantee access to full-day lift tickets, including one-night stays on Saturday, February 20, at the Jordan Hotel and Snow Cap Inn. Please note that kid's ski school lessons are now available to book online with limited availability. 

We require online lift ticket purchase and set limits on the number of tickets sold for each day. This includes full-day, beginner, Threedom additional days, and Passholder Friend tickets. We only expect these limits to affect peak dates, but encourage everyone to buy early to ensure access on the dates you want. Season passes, Ikon passes, and lift ticket packs remain direct-to-lift with no restrictions or reservations.

Fast and contactless lift ticket pickup is available at our PUBs (Pick Up Boxes) at base lodges and hotels. Just scan the barcode at the bottom of your confirmation email and your RFID cards will print with your lift tickets or ticket packs loaded. For best results turn off dark mode on your phone if you use it and avoid email apps that use dark backgrounds.

Please remember to take a self-administered Health Screening and visit our COVID Winter Guide before each trip to stay up-to-date on safety protocols and state guidelines.
