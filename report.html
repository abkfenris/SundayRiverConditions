Weekly Trail Report 

July 14, 2022

It sure feels like summer! Beat the heat and head up to the top of one of Sunday River’s peaks for a cool breeze and stunning views of Western Maine and the White Mountains. Don’t forget to bring plenty of water, sunscreen and a hat, and some salty snacks to keep you hydrated and cool. Many of our trails involve sections on sunny work roads, so be prepared for full-on summer conditions. Water bottle filling stations are located in South Ridge Lodge.

Lowbush blueberries have come into season! Please be respectful of other visitors to the mountain and our wildlife if you choose to pick berries. A good harvesting practice is taking less than 1/10th of what is growing in a particular spot. Plentiful berries also mean that bear activity has increased on the hill. Keep your eye out for our black bear families, and please keep your distance. A good rule of thumb for wildlife encounters is truly a rule of thumb! With your arm outstretched, an animal should appear no bigger than the nail on your thumb, if it looks any larger you are too close!

As we gear up for Tough Mountain Challenge, you may notice some obstacles beginning to take shape around the mountain. Please refrain from testing them out. Obstacles are still incomplete and may not be safe or secured.

Construction is booming up at North Peak! Some trails and the Chondola may be closed for 30-45 minute periods on Thursday (7/14) and Friday (7/15) to allow for safe blasting activities. Please be patient and adhere to all posted signs and warnings. Hiking is allowed only on designated trails. Closed means closed for your own safety.

If you are injured or need assistance at any point, don’t hesitate to contact Patrol at (207)-824-5350 or by visiting our First Aid Clinic in the bottom of South Ridge Lodge next to Betty’s Treats and Treasures.
