Summer Days at the River

July 4, 2022

Summer has landed at the mountain. The Sunday River Golf Course is open and playing beautifully! Tee times are open for the whole summer, so book early to secure the lowest rates. Stay & Play packages are also available to save even more on your next favorite golf vacation. These packages are only available by phone, so call (877) 346-0451 to book. 

Scenic lift rides are officially open for the summer! Join us Thursday - Sunday from 10AM - 4PM for a ride up to the top of North Peak on our Chondola. Enjoy sweeping views of the Mahoosuc Mountains, including Maine's fourth highest peak, Old Speck. Scenic lift rides are dog, kid, and grandparent-friendly, so pack up the whole family. Tickets can be pre-purchased here.

Sunday River Outfitters will be closed this week. Join us when we reopen on July 11 for a guided kayak trip, learn archery for the afternoon, or spend time sprucing up your stand-up paddleboarding skills. Sunday River Outfitters classes are offered Monday through Thursday, based out of the Jordan or Grand Summit Hotel. Learn more and sign up here.

The countdown is on to Tough Mountain Challenge on July 23! Advanced registration is closed, but you can still sign up on the day of the event. Make sure to join us the night before on July 22 for the Big Ass Bash. Is there a little daredevil in your life who wants to take on the challenge? Check out Mini Mountain Challenge on July 24, TMC-style race for kids 12 and under. Registration for Mini Mountain is open until July 22 - sign up here!

Did you forget to purchase your season pass? No worries! All season passes and ticket pack options are back on sale, so you can lock in your winter for less. As always, keep an eye here, and sign up for our newsletter to receive all the important updates right to your inbox. 

Weekly Trail Report 

July 7, 2022

WARNING: There are active blasting and construction sites at the top of the Chondola and on American Express. Exercise caution when traveling on work roads and use a marked hiking trails whenever possible. Three Mile Trail/Trail E may be closed at certain points to allow safe passage of machinery.

Our trails are in tip top shape and ready for your summer adventure! Keep your eye out for the many wildflowers and berries growing around the mountain, but beware, a family of black bears has been roaming the mountain enjoying the berries too. Please respect the wildlife that also call this mountain home and keep a safe distance between you and any animal.

Hankering for a sunny lunch at the summit? Ride the Chondola up to North Peak Top and follow the Summit Traverse/Trail E all the way to the White Cap summit! Enjoy lunch with a view of Mt. Washington before heading back down to the Chondola. If you’re feeling adventurous, you can follow trail J down the side of infamous Shockwave before meandering through the forest to the Grand Summit Hotel. Please note, trail J is rated as extreme due to sections of steep rock that require scrambling and some athletic moves.

We have many new and exciting projects underway at Sunday River, including of the Jordan 8, which means we’ve unfortunately had to close hiking access to Spruce, Aurora, Oz, and Jordan peaks. For your safety, please obey signage at all times and remain aware of your surroundings, especially when choosing to walk on roads. For the next two weeks, the Kids Adventure Trail/Trail H will be routed up Peak Easy and down Quantum Leap to Polaris. Please follow signs.

If you need assistance, get lost, or are injured, don’t hesitate to call Patrol at (207)-824-5350. Patrol will be available 7 days a week during normal operating hours.
