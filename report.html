Morning Report

Well, it took some time, but Spring truly feels like it is moving into Western Maine. After a chilly day yesterday, we're looking at a high approaching 60 today, which will make for some excellent spring skiing.

Unsettled weather moved through the area yesterday afternoon and lasted through the night, which has left the mountain with some interesting conditions this morning. Trails will start with a slight glaze on them but will soften quickly due to the warm temps expected today. In addition to trail conditions, some lifts have experienced icing, which may delay their opening. Crews are on-site and are working diligently to get lifts spinning, but in case of any delays, keep an eye here for all of the latest updates.

It's shaping up to be an excellent run of weather here in Western Maine, and we've got a full slate of awesome Spring events coming at you this weekend. Pond-A-Palooza and our final Spring Concert of the year are on Saturday, and we've got fun Easter events planned for Sunday, so come and celebrate with family and friends at your happy place. For all of the events through the rest of the year, check out the full schedule.

As we wind down for the season be sure to check out our Spring operations schedule for all of the info on what lifts are spinning and when.

See you out there!
-Team Snow

Mountain Notes:

If you're looking to get over to Jordan from South Ridge, ski on over to Spruce Peak Triple and take Sirius > Aludra > Cyclone > State Fair. Kansas will bring you back from Jordan to South Ridge when you're ready to head back East.    

Spring brings with it some of the best conditions of the year, but also the most variable condition. Please note that trail conditions may vary and change rapidly throughout the day.

Please note that in order to access condos off of Ridge Run, you must take Dream Maker and then turn onto Ridge Run. We have operations on 3D cutting over the Ridge Run road and cannot have thru-traffic.
