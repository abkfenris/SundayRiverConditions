MONDAY, MARCH 8

What a day! There isn't a cloud in the sky today as we enjoy skiing and riding across all eight peaks under the endless sun. If you are not at the mountain, it's not too late, get out and soak up every bit of sun until last chair. Not to mention the snow is fun and fast. Tomorrow will look a little different with some snow flurries heading our way but whether it's sunshine or snow, we certainly can't complain. For today, keep working on your mask tan, and believe us when we say it’s definitely the cooler version of a goggle tan.

Tomorrow lifts will start spinning at 9AM. Tonight the groomers will head out once the sun goes down to press out perfect stripes for another day of fast turns tomorrow. Over the weekend snowmakers resurfaced trails like American Express, Risky Business, Ecstasy, Right Stuff, and Sunday Punch, so you won’t want to miss those.

The 2021-22 New England Pass is back on sale, granting access to Sunday River, Loon & Sugarloaf all next year. Current passholders can take advantage of renewal rates and new passholders can start using their pass on March 22 when paid in full. With options that fit your budget and schedule, lock in the lowest rates on next year's pass now through April 30.

We require online purchase of lift tickets and limit the number of tickets sold for each day. This includes full-day, beginner, Threedom additional days, and Passholder Friend tickets. We only expect these limits to affect peak dates, but encourage everyone to buy early to ensure access on the dates you want. Season passes, Ikon passes, and lift ticket packs remain direct-to-lift with no restrictions or reservations.

Fast and contactless lift ticket pickup is available at our PUBs (Pick Up Boxes) at base lodges and hotels. Just scan the barcode at the bottom of your confirmation email and your RFID cards will print with your lift tickets or ticket packs loaded. For best results turn off dark mode on your phone if you use it and avoid email apps that use dark backgrounds.

Please remember to take a self-administered Health Screening and visit our COVID Winter Guide before each trip to stay up-to-date on safety protocols and state guidelines.
