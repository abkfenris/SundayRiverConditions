Snowmaking in September

September 30, 2022

It may just be a test, but still, snowmaking in September? Pinch us. 6 HKD high-efficiency snow guns roared to life this morning at the top of Locke, checking the box on one of the final steps before ski season. When the temperatures drop for good, we'll be ready to go. Will you? Time is running out to save on season passes and ticket packs, with prices increasing, and several products going away for good, at midnight on October 10. While you're browsing, don't forget to check out Lift Tickets, Lodging, and lessons through our Snowsports School. We'll be sliding on snow in no time, so plan now and save big.

Looking ahead, we've got Fall Fest on October 8-9. The North American Wife Carrying Championship returns on Saturday, and the prize is a big one: win the wife's weight in beer, and FIVE TIMES her weight in cash! This classic SR event shouldn't be missed. And don't forget the New England Cornhole Championship on Sunday - 48 teams will compete, but only one will be crowned winner. Looking to stay the weekend? Lock in your lodging now to make sure you've got a place to crash after the festivities. 

Looking for a casual way to enjoy the beautiful scenery of fall in Western Maine?  Join us Saturday & Sunday from 10AM - 4PM for a ride up to the top of North Peak on our Chondola. Enjoy sweeping views of the Mahoosuc Mountains, including Maine's fourth highest peak, Old Speck. Scenic lift rides are dog, kid, and grandparent-friendly, so pack up the whole family. Tickets can be pre-purchased here. 

The Sunday River Golf Club closes for the season on October 10. Tee times are still available, so book early to secure your spot. Stay & Play packages are also available to save even more on your next favorite golf vacation. These packages are only available by phone, so call (877) 346-0451 to book. 

Weekly Trail Report 

September 29, 2022

It's a great time to hit the trails - the foliage is here! Head to the top of one of Sunday River's peaks for a cool breeze and phenomenal views of Western Maine, the White Mountains, and the Mahoosuc Mountains. Don't forget to bring plenty of water, sunscreen, a hat, suitable footwear, some layers, and some salty snacks to replace what you sweat out. Water bottle filling stations are located in South Ridge Lodge, as well as the hotels. The mountain received lots of heavy rain this week, so please be wary of washed-out trails. 

Construction is nearly wrapped up on North Peak, but there may still be some heavy machinery in that area from time to time. Please be wary of large holes and don't hike alone if you are unsure of your abilities. If you are having trouble finding your way, please ask one of our knowledgeable lift operators or patrollers and they will be able to assist you! 

Looking to take a trail less traveled? Trail B, accessible from the Jordan Hotel, sees far less foot traffic than some of our other trails. Please be aware of construction surrounding this trail, however, and please adhere to all posted signs and warnings. Do not cross orange ropes or enter areas beyond “no trespassing” or “trail closed due to construction” signs.

There are plenty of tables and chairs around North Peak lodge for you to enjoy a picnic lunch or sunny siesta with a view, but please remember to dispose of your trash and recycling at the top or bottom of the lift.

Construction continues on Barker Mountain Rd. During the week, Monday - Friday, there will be limited access depending where the crew is working. Most access will be allowed via Cascade Hotel Hill Dr. Weekend traffic will flow as normal.

If you are injured or need assistance at any point, please contact Patrol at (207)-824-5350 or visit our First Aid Clinic in the bottom of the South Ridge Lodge next to Betty's Treats and Treasures.
