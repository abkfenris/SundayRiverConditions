Afternoon Report

It's official, spring's here. Low-lying clouds and drizzle last night made for an interesting morning, but thanks to the hard work of our Mountain Ops team, and some warming from Mother Nature, we're having ourselves a good 'ol day of spring skiing. Temps are expected to stay mild throughout the night, which will make for delightful soft turns for first chair. 

Grooming will take to the hill tonight to resurface their standard trails in need. Please note, high winds are forecasted throughout the day and may impact lift operations. As always, keep an eye on the Mountain Report for the most up-to-date operational info.

After tomorrow's potential wind, it's shaping up to be an excellent run of weather here in Western Maine, and we've got a full slate of awesome Spring events coming at you this weekend. Pond-A-Palooza and our final Spring Concert of the year are on Saturday, and we've got fun Easter events planned for Sunday, so come and celebrate with family and friends at your happy place. For all of the events through the rest of the year, check out the full schedule.

As we wind down for the season be sure to check out our Spring operations schedule for all of the info on what lifts are spinning and when.

See you out there!
-Team Snow

Mountain Notes:

If you're looking to get over to Jordan from South Ridge, ski on over to Spruce Peak Triple and take Sirius > Aludra > Cyclone > State Fair. Kansas will bring you back from Jordan to South Ridge when you're ready to head back East.    

Spring brings with it some of the best conditions of the year, but also the most variable condition. Please note that trail conditions may vary and change rapidly throughout the day.

Please note that in order to access condos off of Ridge Run, you must take Dream Maker and then turn onto Ridge Run. We have operations on 3D cutting over the Ridge Run road and cannot have thru-traffic.
