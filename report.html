TUESDAY, FEBRUARY 16

Alright, who was it? Someone forgot to snow dance last night and Mother Nature was not impressed. Instead of the huge storm we were hoping for she sent us 1-2” of snow and wintery mix. Luckily for us, we have a second chance. Thursday into Friday we have storm number two coming in, and I think we are owed a good one. For today, you can enjoy slashing up soft snow and carving playful corduroy. The snowcats spent the night laying down stripes across all eight peaks but left trails like Black Hole, South Paw, and Quantum Leap ungroomed along with our other natural terrain. If you’re just learning, start the day at South Ridge for the best beginner terrain. Groomers hit those trails last and left behind nice soft corduroy.

Lifts open at 8AM every day this week. Looking ahead we have sunshine the next couple of days and a snowstorm to end the week. Twilight skiing will continue tonight through Saturday for Presidents’ Week with access to the Chondola and South Ridge Express until 6:30PM. As you plan your trip, keep in mind lift tickets are limited and days are close to selling out, so plan ahead if you want to hit the slopes this week. Or book lodging with us and guarantee access to full-day lift tickets, including one-night stays through Thursday, February 18, at the Jordan Hotel and Snow Cap Inn. Please note that kids ski school lessons are now available to book online with limited availability.

We require online lift ticket purchase and set limits on the number of tickets sold for each day. This includes full-day, beginner, Threedom additional days, and Passholder Friend tickets. We only expect these limits to affect peak dates, but encourage everyone to buy early to ensure access on the dates you want. Season passes, Ikon passes, and lift ticket packs remain direct-to-lift with no restrictions or reservations.

Fast and contactless lift ticket pickup is available at our PUBs (Pick Up Boxes) at base lodges and hotels. Just scan the barcode at the bottom of your confirmation email and your RFID cards will print with your lift tickets or ticket packs loaded. For best results turn off dark mode on your phone if you use it and avoid email apps that use dark backgrounds.

Please remember to take a self-administered Health Screening and visit our COVID Winter Guide before each trip to stay up-to-date on safety protocols and state guidelines.

Masks up!
