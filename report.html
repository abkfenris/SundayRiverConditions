December 4, 2021

Three words: major. terrain. expansion! Today, we opened American Express off of the Spruce Peak Triple, and fired up the Chondola for the first day of the season, opening Escapade, Grand Rapids, and Lower Downdraft along with it. We're heading into Sunday Funday with the most open terrain in the East, and we're not stopping there. The snowmakers will continue their wintry efforts on American Express, Sirius, Aludra, Lights Out, Lower Downdraft, Grand Rapids, and Escapade tonight, while the snowcats will be doing their nightly rounds of corduroy. Plus, don't miss the new park features in Wonderland, as well as the terrain park on Upper Sunday Punch if you're feeling sendy. 

Tomorrow, we'll be spinning five lifts from Locke to South Ridge from 8AM-3:45PM, and it's going to feel very jolly on the slopes with the 21st Annual Santa Sunday event. Registration is open online to be one of 250+ Saint Nicks skiing down the Broadway trail around 11AM tomorrow, all for a great cause. 

All lift tickets need to be purchased online in advance. Have your RFID from last year? You can head straight to the lift! Skier services are available in both the Barker and South Ridge Lodges, as well as shuttle service between these lodges and the hotels while the lifts spin. We are now accepting cashless forms of payment only. Please be sure to have a gift card, resort charge, credit card, or debit card available for on-site purchases.

Around the Resort

The South Ridge Food Court is now open, along with the Barker Food Court, Waffle Cabin in Barker Basin, Northern Lights Cafe at the Jordan Hotel, Camp at the Grand Summit Hotel, and Shipyard Brew Haus at White Cap Lodge. Foggy Goggle opens for the season tomorrow! Plus, get your retail therapy in at Sunday River Sports, and your relaxation on at the Jordan Spa. Please note that the Tune Shop is closed this weekend, but you can pick up skis at the repair counter in Sunday River Sports.

For the latest updates whenever you visit us, head to the River Guide.

COVID-19 Protocols

Though we are no longer requiring guests to wear masks in general areas, we suggest masking indoors. However, some services may require you to wear a mask, so be sure to have one on hand. Please make sure to read through our COVID Winter Guide before visiting the resort to understand our operating plans and how they may affect you. Remember, we're all in this together.

Carve on!
