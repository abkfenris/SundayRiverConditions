Afternoon Report
Last Updated: February 23, 2024 | 1:34 PM

Temps sure did warm up today, and we're currently sitting in the low forties. The sun is out, the turns are soft, and it's a great day to check out our slopeside decks for some après. A few trails opened throughout the day, including Lost Princess, bringing us to over 745 acres of open terrain. Today's the perfect opportunity to get started on those goggle tans. 

Snowmaking will pause until Monday night with these warmer temps, but Mother Nature gave us a little assistance this morning. Our grooming team will be out and about tonight, and they'll be grooming the same list of trails as last night. As of this time, the Oz Quad is scheduled to spin tomorrow. The Quantum Leap Triple and the Jordan Mountain Double will both open at 8AM tomorrow. The Aurora Quad will remain on hold following a mechanical issue. Lower Airglow, Celestial, and Supernova will be closed, but Northern Lights, Lower Vortex, Paradigm, and Sensation will remain open.

It's never a bad time to review the Skier Responsibility Code. Take a moment, refresh yourself before heading out, and please remember to go slow in the base areas.

Presidents' Week schedule remains jam-packed. Tonight, we have a fire dancing performance at South Ridge at 6:15PM and 6:45PM. We have twilight skiing tonight as well, so stick around and ski under the lights until 6:30PM. Afterward, head to the Grand Summit Hotel to check out the Après Aglow Lighted Trail from 4:30-8:30PM. We have live music today at the Foggy Goggle, so swing by to see Hit the Bus play from 3-6PM.  We also have a few spots open for the new après experience at the Alpeniglu at Sunday River for tomorrow night, so grab your tickets before they're gone. Still deciding what to do this vacation week? We have single-night stays at all resort properties through the rest of this week, so be sure to check that out.

We'll see you out there!

- Team Snow
